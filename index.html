Section 2 of 5

The Continuous Loop

Runs hundreds of times per second. Every robot follows this same cycle.

↻ Repeat — never stops while the robot runs
5-Step Process — Runs 100s of Times/Second
01
Sense — Cameras, LiDAR, GPS, touch & temp gather real-world data continuously
02
Think — Controller software answers: obstacle ahead? safe path? emergency stop?
03
Plan — Calculates optimal path, speed, torque & timing before acting
04
Act — Motors, hydraulics & servos execute with precision across dozens of joints
05
Feedback — "Did I land correctly?" Sensors measure result and correct instantly

Robotics = automation in the physical world. Your trading algorithm senses data, thinks, and acts. A robot does the same — but in three dimensions.

The key bridge between software and robotics

5 Driving Forces Behind Rapid Adoption
💵
Economics
Robot prices down ~50% since 2010. A Universal Robots cobot costs ~$35K and pays for itself in under 2 years at $20/hr labor rates
🎯
Precision at Scale
Micron-level consistency across millions of repetitions. Human workers fatigue — robots don't drift after hour 8
🛡️
Workplace Safety
Removes people from chemical plants, mines, extreme heat & repetitive-strain roles. 340 warehouse workers die annually in the US
⏰
24/7 Operation
No breaks, no sick days, no overtime. Lights-out factories — fully automated with no humans on the floor — already exist
📦
Post-COVID Acceleration
Supply-chain fragility compressed 5 years of automation investment into 18 months. Goldman Sachs confirmed the pull-forward

Four Theological Questions Robotics Forces Us to Answer
👑
Human Dignity
Genesis 1:27 — mankind bears God's image. Robotics asks: does intelligence in a machine confer moral standing? The church must answer carefully and clearly
⚖️
Justice for Workers
Proverbs 14:31 — "Whoever oppresses the poor shows contempt for their Maker." Automation that displaces low-wage workers without redistribution mirrors what the prophets condemned
🌿
Creation Stewardship
Technology is never neutral. Robots can restore coral reefs or accelerate resource extraction. The question is always: toward what end, and for whom? (Genesis 2:15)
⚡
Accountability Gap
When Uber's self-driving car killed a pedestrian in 2018, prosecutors couldn't find one person to charge. Scripture insists persons must answer for systems they build (Romans 13)

Genesis 2:18 — "It is not good for man to be alone." God's answer is human covenant community, not a robot companion
